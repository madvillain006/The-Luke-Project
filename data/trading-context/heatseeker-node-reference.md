## Bot Context Summary

- Heatseeker maps describe dealer-positioning nodes. Treat as interpretive confluence, not a standalone trade trigger.
- Focus on absolute node value more than sign or color. Higher absolute value means stronger pull, pin, rejection, or volatility effect.
- Yellow nodes dampen price movement, behave like magnets/pins, and often form reversal or range edges.
- Purple nodes amplify movement and volatility. Purple zones can create fast moves once price enters, and may also act as reversal/deflection zones.
- Light green nodes are ambiguous; watch for green nodes turning yellow.
- King Nodes are the highest absolute value nodes. They often matter more than other map signals and can become end-of-day pin targets.
- Gatekeeper Nodes sit between spot and King Nodes. They can reject price, reshuffle the map, and create high-probability directional shifts.
- Midpoints inside a range are poor directional R:R. Prefer waiting for range edges and fading extremes when context supports it.
- Fresh untouched nodes deserve more respect than repeatedly tested nodes. Each touch usually weakens bounce/rejection odds unless the node grows again.
- Node rate of change matters: rapid accumulation strengthens magnet behavior; rapid unwinding weakens prior levels.
- Rolling ceilings lower is bearish evidence. Rolling floors higher is bullish evidence.
- Robinhood power-hour liquidation around 3:30 PM ET can create forced flow, fakeouts, breakouts, volatility spikes, and map reshuffles.
- Hedge Nodes around macro events are often far from spot and slow-moving. Treat them as protective positioning, not active magnets by default.
- Air pockets are low-volume/low-node zones price can move through, but only with broader confluence.
- OPEX week distorts node reliability. Treat levels with caution until post-OPEX clarity returns.
- SPX node interaction margin is roughly 5-10 points; do not expect exact-cent rejection.

## Full Source Notes

### Understanding the Market Participants

To make sense of what Heatseeker is showing, understand the main options-market players.

Market Dealers are liquidity providers. Their job is to take the opposite side of trader positions, hedge risk, and profit from spreads and volatility. Their hedging can create pressure that influences price movement.

Institutions and Funds are large entities such as mutual funds, pension funds, and hedge funds. Their options activity is usually deliberate and can anchor key market levels.

Customers include retail traders and smaller professional traders. Their flow can be unpredictable, but crowded customer flow can stress dealers and force hedging.

### How to Use Heatseeker

Trending day:
If price is moving smoothly toward a major node with no strong opposing node, be ready for volatility expansion. Momentum trading works best in these moments.

Choppy or range-bound day:
If price is trapped between two yellow zones, expect low volatility and fakeouts. This favors premium-selling strategies or reversal strategies such as fading range edges.

High-volatility breakouts:
Watch price approaching purple zones with speed. Once inside, expect fast moves. A major purple node below current price can create an aggressive bounce.

### Node Color Concepts

Bright yellow nodes represent price areas where dealers absorb price movement. They reduce volatility and often act like magnetic pillows where price slows, pins, or reverses.

Dark purple nodes represent areas where dealer positioning amplifies price movement. They increase volatility and can create fast spikes or drops once price enters. They can also act as reversal zones.

Each node has a number. The higher the absolute value, regardless of sign, the stronger the pull or explosion.

### Margin of Interaction

Nodes do not always reject to the exact cent. Expect a deflection margin around 5-10 points on SPX.

### King Nodes

King Nodes are the highest absolute-value nodes on the heatmap. They represent where market makers hold the greatest exposure and where price often gravitates near expiration.

There can be multiple significant nodes. Strong nodes can pull in opposite directions, creating range-bound pinning or whipsaw movement.

End-of-day pin jobs:
Market makers often pin price near the King Node late in the session. Tight ranges form. Scalping range edges tends to work best.

Early-day drives away:
When price reaches a King Node too early, market makers may push it away because holding price there all session requires constant defense.

### Gatekeeper Nodes

Gatekeeper Nodes act like bouncers between price and the King Node. They can prevent price from easily reaching the King Node and can cause major directional shifts.

If price tests and fails at a Gatekeeper Node, the map can reshuffle. Reshuffles often precede trend changes or dealer pin realignment. Gatekeeper rejections near the start of the day often mark high-probability reversals.

After rejection, reshuffles occur and the next directional bias can become clearer.

### Midpoints

Midpoints are dealer trap zones and often have poor directional risk-to-reward. Unless using option-selling or spread-selling strategies, avoid trading them.

Direction is uncertain in the middle of the range, while upper and lower heatmap ranges are easier to see. Better strategy: wait for price to approach a range edge and fade the edge when context supports it.

### Price Delivery and Node Retests

When looking for bounce plays off a node, context matters. If price has already delivered from a node, meaning touched and moved away, its influence weakens. Each additional test reduces the likelihood of a strong bounce.

When price bounces or rejects from a Gatekeeper or King Node, it does not always revert back to that node. A touched node is no longer fresh, and the probability of reversion back to it can drop.

If the node decreases after delivery, return to that node becomes lower probability. If the node increases after delivery, reversion back to that node becomes more likely.

### Robinhood Power Hour Liquidation

At 3:30 PM ET, 30 minutes before market close, Robinhood begins auto-liquidating accounts that fail margin requirements. This can create forced order flow in liquid names like SPX, SPY, and QQQ.

It can trigger volatility spikes, force breakouts or fakeouts near Gatekeeper Nodes, accelerate moves near a King Node, and sometimes reshuffle the map.

### Rate of Change of a Node

Heatseeker shows dealer positioning and how fast liquidity changes.

Rapid accumulation means dealers are quickly adding exposure and the node can act like a stronger magnet.

Rapid unwinding means exposure vanishes and levels that looked strong may weaken.

Fast changes often cause volatility spikes, explosive moves, or sharp reversals.

Rolling ceilings:
If upside ceilings or upside price targets decrease and shift to a lower strike, that is strong presumptive bearish evidence.

Rolling floors:
If downside floors or downside price targets decrease while the floor moves to a higher strike, that is strong presumptive bullish evidence.

### Hedge Nodes

Hedge Nodes appear during macro events such as FOMC, CPI, JOLTS, NFP, or earnings. They represent large protective positions farther from price and usually move slowly.

They are typically static or slow-unwinding, may appear above and below price at once, and function like insurance rather than active magnets.

### Air Pockets

Air Pockets occur when maps show low volume or small nodes that price can move through because there is little resistance. Context matters. Technical analysis and confluence across heatmaps in Trinity mode affect whether price can actually move through the pocket.

### OPEX Nodes

Every third Friday of the month is OPEX week. Nodes may carry less weight because contracts expire, positions roll off or reset, and dealer positioning can temporarily distort. After OPEX week, directional bias and map clarity often improve.

Treat OPEX week levels with caution because probabilities are temporarily skewed.

### Summary

- Focus on absolute value, not color or sign alone.
- Favor untouched nodes over retested ones.
- Watch rate of change for momentum clues.
- Avoid midpoints.
- Respect Gatekeepers.
- Track King Nodes daily.
- Be mindful of external catalysts like Power Hour, OPEX, and Hedge Nodes.
