What Changed Architecturally

1. From single cockpit to front shell

Jarvis mostly lived through `chat.html`. Luke now has `/shell` as the front door, with drilldowns for Daily, Radar, trading, brain status, Operator V2, and replay windows. The recent Radar work also removed duplicate visible launch paths in the front UI.

2. From execution confidence to safety proof

Jarvis had human gates, but the UI still carried live/staged execution affordances. Luke increasingly says: read-only, replay, proof first. Current docs explicitly block live readiness without separate market data and broker proof.

3. From root-state soup to registered paths

Jarvis root included many JSON/JSONL runtime files. Luke uses `lib/paths.js`, `state/events`, `state/snapshots`, `data`, and ignored artifacts. Not glamorous, but neither is labeling the breaker box. You still do it.

4. From scaffold agents to goal protocol

Architect/Sweeper were early self-maintenance scaffolds. Luke now adds `.agent` plans, proof scripts, regression tests, and source-of-truth docs. The scaffolding is less "AI, please freestyle" and more "AI, here is the checklist; try not to invent a basement."

5. From trading-only to companion lanes

Daily, History/Career, Automation Business, Developer Stack, Luke System, and Radar show the broader companion direction. Trading is still important, but it has roommates now, and some of them pay rent.

6. From source capture to synthesis

Jarvis had Discord, Kat, Bobby, Ximes, research, and manual paste workflows. Luke adds Radar as the front-facing intake layer: capture raw sources, preserve provenance, classify, summarize, and feed review queues without claiming magic trading certainty.

The honest joke: Jarvis was "what if we bolted a jet engine to a spreadsheet?" Luke is "what if the jet engine had a dashboard, brakes, and a person with veto power?"

Presentation Readout

The story in one minute

Jarvis began as a fast, practical assistant for trading, research, and life operations. It quickly grew agents, workflows, confluence parsing, live/paper/shadow trading paths, and a lot of root-level state. Then the project hit the obvious wall: if this thing is going to matter, it cannot be a pile of clever scripts with a steering wheel taped on. Luke is the consolidation: local front shell, separated surfaces, explicit proof scripts, read-only default trading, organized state, and companion lanes like Daily and Radar. The project is not smaller now. It is safer, more presentable, and less likely to bite the person feeding it.

What I would say confidently

  • The current source of truth is `C:\Users\conor\luke`, not `jarvis`.
  • Jarvis explains the origin and design DNA.
  • Scaffolding evolved from self-mutating agents into goal plans, proof scripts, and tests.
  • The front UI is now the Luke shell, with backend dashboards as drilldowns.
  • Trading is intentionally human-gated and should stay that way until a separate proof phase says otherwise.

What I would not oversell

  • I would not claim Jarvis migration is historically clean. It is not.
  • I would not claim all residue is gone. It is not, and some residue is evidence.
  • I would not claim live trading readiness. The repo itself says no.
  • I would not claim docs are fully current after May 7. Radar is newer than the May 5 status doc.
  • I would not trust any single dashboard as the whole truth. Use tests, routes, and git history together.
